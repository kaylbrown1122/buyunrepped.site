Closing costs can catch first-time buyers off guard. In Tennessee, buyers typically pay between 2-5% of the purchase price in closing costs. Here's exactly what to expect and how to potentially reduce these fees.

## What Are Closing Costs?

Closing costs are the fees and expenses you pay when finalizing your home purchase, separate from your down payment. These costs compensate various parties involved in the transaction.

## Typical Tennessee Closing Costs

### Lender Fees
- **Origination Fee**: 0.5-1% of loan amount
- **Application Fee**: $300-$500
- **Credit Report Fee**: $25-$50
- **Underwriting Fee**: $400-$800

### Title and Escrow
- **Title Search**: $200-$400
- **Title Insurance**: $1,000-$2,500 (based on purchase price)
- **Escrow Fee**: $500-$1,000
- **Recording Fees**: $100-$200

### Property-Related
- **Home Inspection**: $300-$500
- **Appraisal**: $400-$600
- **Survey**: $300-$500 (if required)

### Government Fees
- **Transfer Tax**: Tennessee charges $0.37 per $100 of purchase price
- **Recording Tax**: Varies by county

### Prepaid Items
- **Homeowner's Insurance**: First year premium
- **Property Taxes**: Prorated amount
- **Prepaid Interest**: Interest from closing to first payment

## How to Reduce Closing Costs

### 1. Shop Around for Lenders
Different lenders charge different fees. Get at least three quotes and compare the Loan Estimate documents.

### 2. Negotiate with the Seller
In Tennessee, it's common to ask sellers to contribute toward closing costs, especially in a buyer's market.

### 3. Time Your Closing
Closing at the end of the month reduces prepaid interest charges.

### 4. Skip Unnecessary Add-ons
Some fees are optional. Review each line item and question what you don't understand.

## What You Can't Avoid

Some costs are non-negotiable:
- Government transfer taxes and recording fees
- Title insurance (highly recommended)
- Lender-required fees for your loan type
