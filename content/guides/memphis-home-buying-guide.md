Memphis is the most affordable major housing market in Tennessee. The median home price in Shelby County sits around $190,000, which means buyer's agent commissions run smaller in absolute dollars than in Nashville or Knoxville.<sup>1</sup> But the savings from going unrepresented are still real: 2.42% of $190,000 is $4,598 in commission that could instead cover your entire closing cost obligation, or go directly toward your down payment.<sup>2</sup>

Memphis also has the highest property tax rate of any major Tennessee county. Shelby County's effective rate means buyers need to understand exactly what they're getting into on carrying costs, not just purchase price. This guide covers both: the unrepresented buying process in Memphis and the local financial realities that affect what ownership actually costs here.

## Memphis's Housing Market in 2026

Memphis has always been one of the most accessible entry points into Tennessee homeownership. The market's current conditions favor buyers more than they have in several years.

Key conditions:<sup>1</sup><sup>3</sup>

- Median sale price in Shelby County: approximately $190,000
- Days on market: 45–75 days on average, with higher-end properties sitting longer
- Inventory: expanding across most price ranges
- Price reductions: common, particularly above $250,000

Memphis is not a market where you need to waive contingencies or offer dramatically above list to win. In most price ranges and neighborhoods, you have time to be thoughtful, do your research, and negotiate from a position of information rather than panic.

## Memphis Neighborhoods: A Buyer's Orientation

Memphis is a large, geographically spread city with significant variation in price, condition, and character across neighborhoods. Here's a practical orientation for buyers doing their own research.

### Inner-City Neighborhoods

- **Midtown**: The cultural and social center of Memphis. Walkable, dense, older housing stock including bungalows, Craftsman homes, and Victorian-era properties. Prices range from $150,000 for fixer-uppers to $400,000+ for renovated homes.
- **Cooper-Young**: A walkable neighborhood within Midtown, restaurant and retail district. Higher price points than surrounding Midtown areas. Popular with first-time buyers willing to pay for walkability.
- **Central Gardens**: One of Midtown's most desirable historic districts. Large older homes, significant price premiums. Entry-level starts around $400,000.
- **Vollentine-Evergreen (VECA)**: An active neighborhood association area within Midtown, more affordable than Cooper-Young, active renovation market.

### East Memphis

- **East Memphis proper**: More suburban character than Midtown, larger lots, good schools, established neighborhoods. Prices typically $250,000–$500,000.
- **Germantown**: Technically a separate city within Shelby County, not Memphis proper. Excellent schools, low crime, high demand. Entry-level around $300,000, much higher for larger homes.
- **Collierville**: Another separate Shelby County municipality with its own tax rate and school system. Higher price points, newer construction.

### South and North Memphis

- **Whitehaven**: South Memphis, historically Black neighborhood, affordable entry points. Active investor market, which means you'll compete with cash buyers on lower-priced properties.
- **Raleigh / Frayser**: North Memphis, most affordable area of the city. Higher investor activity, longer days on market, more distressed inventory.

### Shelby County Suburbs

- **Bartlett**: Popular suburb in northeast Shelby County. Family-oriented, good schools, newer housing stock. Prices $200,000–$350,000.
- **Cordova**: Eastern Shelby County. Mix of established neighborhoods and newer construction. Strong school options.
- **Arlington**: Fastest-growing area of Shelby County. New construction, expanding infrastructure, lower prices than comparable Germantown/Collierville product.

## Understanding Shelby County's Property Tax Rate

This is the most important financial fact for Memphis buyers: **Shelby County has the highest property tax rate of any major county in Tennessee.**

The combined Shelby County and City of Memphis property tax rate is approximately $3.39 per $100 of assessed value for properties within Memphis city limits.<sup>5</sup> Tennessee assesses residential properties at 25% of appraised value.

Here's what that means in practice:

| Home Price | Assessed Value (25%) | Annual Property Tax (City) | Annual Property Tax (Suburban) |
|---|---|---|---|
| $150,000 | $37,500 | ~$1,271 | ~$900–$1,100 |
| $190,000 | $47,500 | ~$1,610 | ~$1,140–$1,390 |
| $250,000 | $62,500 | ~$2,119 | ~$1,500–$1,830 |
| $350,000 | $87,500 | ~$2,966 | ~$2,100–$2,563 |

Properties in Germantown, Collierville, Bartlett, and other Shelby County municipalities have their own tax rates, generally lower than the combined city/county rate but still among the highest in Tennessee. Always verify the exact tax rate for the specific municipality and county before you make an offer.

Your lender will escrow 2–3 months of property taxes at closing plus prorated taxes through year-end. On a $190,000 Memphis city property, budget $400–$600 for property tax escrow deposits at closing.

## Step 1: Build Your Support Team

### Hire a Memphis Real Estate Attorney

Tennessee doesn't require an attorney at closing, but for unrepresented buyers, hiring one is your most important protective move.<sup>6</sup> A Memphis real estate attorney typically charges $600–$1,200 for a standard residential closing, including contract review and closing representation.

What they do: review and negotiate contract language, verify contingency terms, and represent your interests at closing. What they don't do: find homes, schedule showings, or provide market guidance.

### Get Pre-Approved With a Lender

Get quotes from at least three lenders before you start making offers. In Memphis's price range, the difference between lenders can be $2,000–$4,000 in origination fees and rate pricing. Local banks and credit unions often have competitive rates in Memphis's market.

When you're talking to lenders, confirm the seller concession limits for your loan type. FHA loans allow up to 6% seller contributions toward your costs. Conventional loans cap it at 3%–6% depending on down payment. In Memphis's market, seller concessions are common and worth asking for. Our [seller concessions guide](/resources/seller-concessions-tennessee-guide) explains how to structure the request.

### Line Up a Home Inspector

Memphis-area inspections typically run $300–$400 for a standard home. Specific concerns in the Memphis market:

- **Foundation issues**: Memphis sits on expansive clay soils, and parts of the metro sit in a seismic zone (New Madrid Fault). Foundation concerns are more prevalent here than in other Tennessee markets.
- **Older electrical**: Pre-1960s homes in Midtown and older suburbs frequently have dated wiring (fuse boxes, two-prong outlets, aluminum wiring) that affects insurance rates and requires upgrades.
- **HVAC**: Memphis's hot, humid summers are hard on cooling systems. Any unit older than 10 years should be evaluated.
- **Drainage and flooding**: Low-lying areas of Shelby County have drainage challenges. Ask specifically about basement and crawlspace moisture.

## Step 2: Search Memphis's Market

Zillow, Redfin, and Realtor.com show virtually all Memphis MLS listings in real time. One additional source worth checking: Memphis Area Association of Realtors members often list on local platforms first, and the regional MLS updates to national aggregators within hours.

Open houses are common in Memphis and a good way to tour properties without signing a buyer-broker agreement. For private showings, contact the listing agent directly and identify yourself as an unrepresented buyer. Tennessee law requires the listing agent to verbally disclose their role and confirm it in writing before preparing any offer on your behalf (TCA 62-13-405).<sup>7</sup>

**Investor competition**: In Memphis's lower price ranges (under $150,000), you'll compete with cash investors. If you're financing your purchase, get your pre-approval letter in order before you start searching in this range, and be prepared for sellers who prefer cash offers, especially on distressed properties.

## Step 3: Research Comparable Sales in Memphis

Shelby County's wide neighborhood variation means you need to pull comps carefully. A home in Cooper-Young and a home two miles away in a different neighborhood can differ by $100/square foot or more.

Use the Shelby County Assessor's database for tax assessment history and prior sale records. Use Redfin's "sold" filter to find closed sales within the past 6 months, within 0.5 miles, matching square footage, bedroom count, and condition.

Homes listed above $250,000 in Memphis sit longer and reduce more frequently. If you're shopping in that range, days on market is a signal worth tracking.

## Step 4: Make an Offer

### Setting Your Offer Price

In most Memphis price ranges, you have room to offer below list on properties that have been on market 30+ days. The Memphis market isn't the kind of environment where most properties get multiple offers above asking. Do your comp research, make an evidence-based offer, and let your attorney review the contract language before you submit.

### Contingencies to Include

Include financing, inspection, appraisal, and title contingencies on every offer. In Memphis's market, there's no competitive pressure that justifies waiving them. For a full look at what to watch in the purchase agreement, see our post on [purchase agreement red flags Tennessee buyers often miss](/resources/purchase-agreement-red-flags).

Earnest money in Shelby County typically runs 1%–2% of the purchase price. On a $190,000 home, that's $1,900–$3,800.<sup>3</sup>

## Step 5: Memphis Closing Costs

On a $190,000 purchase in Shelby County, total closing costs typically run $3,800–$9,500 (2%–5% of purchase price). Here's the breakdown of the major line items.<sup>8</sup>

### Tennessee State Taxes (Fixed)

Both of Tennessee's recordation taxes apply at the same statewide rates:

- **Realty transfer tax**: $3.70 per $1,000. On $190,000: $703.
- **Mortgage recordation tax**: $1.15 per $1,000 of loan amount (first $2,000 exempt). On a $180,500 loan (5% down): $205.

Neither can be negotiated or shifted to the seller.<sup>9</sup>

### Lender and Title Fees

Loan origination fees (0.5%–1.5% of loan amount), underwriting fees ($950–$1,250), and title fees are where your leverage lives. Shop at least three lenders and compare Loan Estimates side by side. On a $190,000 purchase, a 1% origination fee difference saves $1,900.

In Shelby County, local customs on title insurance responsibility (who pays the owner's policy) are less standardized than in Middle Tennessee. Confirm who pays in your purchase agreement. Negotiating seller coverage of the owner's title policy ($900–$1,200 on a $190,000 home) is worth asking for.

For the full fee breakdown and negotiability assessment, see our [Tennessee closing costs guide](/resources/tennessee-closing-costs-fees-guide).

## Step 6: Down Payment Assistance in Memphis

Memphis buyers have access to several assistance programs. First-time buyer definitions are often broader than expected, typically meaning you haven't owned a home in the past three years.

### THDA Great Choice Home Loan

Statewide first-time buyer program. Up to 6% down payment and closing cost assistance (maximum $15,000) through Great Choice Plus. Minimum 640 credit score. Income and purchase price limits apply.<sup>10</sup>

In Shelby County, income limits and purchase price limits are set at levels reflecting local market conditions. Check thda.org for current figures.

### City of Memphis and Shelby County Programs

The Memphis and Shelby County governments periodically offer down payment assistance programs funded through HUD Community Development Block Grants. These programs open and close based on funding cycles. Contact Memphis Housing Authority or Shelby County government directly for current availability.

### FHA Loans and Memphis Pricing

At Memphis's median price point, FHA's 3.5% minimum down payment (for buyers with 580+ credit scores) means you can buy a $190,000 home with as little as $6,650 down. FHA also allows sellers to contribute up to 6% of the purchase price toward your closing costs, which on a $190,000 home is $11,400. That can effectively cover all closing costs for a buyer who negotiates it properly. For a full look at available loan programs, see our post on [Tennessee loan programs for first-time buyers](/resources/tennessee-loan-programs-2026).
