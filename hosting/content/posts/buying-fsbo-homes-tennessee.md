Buying a For Sale By Owner (FSBO) home can save you thousands of dollars, but it requires understanding the process. Here's everything you need to know about purchasing FSBO properties in Tennessee.

## Why Consider FSBO Properties?

When sellers list their homes without an agent, they're typically looking to save on commission fees—and that savings can be passed on to you as the buyer. In Tennessee, the average buyer's agent commission is around 2.5-3%, which on a $400,000 home translates to $10,000-$12,000.

## Understanding Tennessee Real Estate Law

Tennessee has specific requirements for real estate transactions that protect both buyers and sellers:

- **Disclosure Requirements**: Sellers must complete the Tennessee Residential Property Disclosure form
- **Title Insurance**: While not required by law, it's strongly recommended for FSBO transactions
- **Attorney Involvement**: Tennessee doesn't require attorney involvement, but having one review your purchase agreement is wise

## Steps to Buying a FSBO Home

### 1. Get Pre-Approved for Financing

Before you start house hunting, secure a mortgage pre-approval. This shows sellers you're a serious buyer and gives you clarity on your budget.

### 2. Research the Property

Without an agent providing comps, you'll need to do your own research:
- Check recent sales in the neighborhood
- Review property tax records
- Look up any liens or encumbrances

### 3. Make Your Offer

Your offer should include:
- Purchase price
- Earnest money amount
- Contingencies (inspection, financing, appraisal)
- Proposed closing date

### 4. Conduct Inspections

Never skip the home inspection. In Tennessee, common issues include:
- Foundation problems due to clay soil
- HVAC concerns in extreme weather
- Roof condition from hail damage

### 5. Navigate to Closing

Work with a title company or real estate attorney to handle:
- Title search and insurance
- Document preparation
- Escrow management
- Recording of the deed

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Skipping Due Diligence**: Just because there's no agent doesn't mean you should skip any steps
2. **Overpaying**: Research comparable sales thoroughly
3. **Weak Contracts**: Use proper Tennessee real estate forms
4. **Missing Deadlines**: Track all contingency periods carefully
