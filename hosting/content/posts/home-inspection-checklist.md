A thorough home inspection is your best protection against costly surprises. Here's what every Tennessee home buyer should know before, during, and after the inspection.

## Before the Inspection

### Choosing an Inspector
- Verify they're licensed by the Tennessee Home Inspector Licensing Board
- Check reviews and ask for sample reports
- Confirm they carry errors and omissions insurance
- Budget $350-$500 for a standard inspection

### What to Have Ready
- Your purchase agreement with inspection contingency dates
- Access to the property (coordinate with seller)
- A list of specific concerns about the home

## During the Inspection

Always attend your home inspection. This is your chance to learn about the home's systems firsthand.

### Exterior Checklist
- **Roof**: Age, condition, missing shingles, flashing
- **Gutters**: Proper drainage, no damage
- **Siding**: Cracks, rot, or damage
- **Foundation**: Cracks, settling, moisture intrusion
- **Grading**: Land slopes away from house
- **Driveway/Walkways**: Condition, cracks

### Interior Checklist
- **Electrical**: Panel condition, GFCI outlets, wiring type
- **Plumbing**: Water pressure, drain speed, water heater age
- **HVAC**: System age, condition, filter access
- **Windows**: Seals, operation, condensation
- **Walls/Ceilings**: Cracks, stains, signs of water damage
- **Floors**: Level, squeaks, condition

### Systems to Verify
- Smoke and carbon monoxide detectors
- Garage door safety features
- Appliance function (if included in sale)

## Tennessee-Specific Concerns

### Foundation Issues
Tennessee's clay soil expands and contracts with moisture, causing foundation movement. Look for:
- Diagonal cracks in drywall
- Doors and windows that stick
- Uneven floors
- Gaps between walls and ceiling

### Termite Damage
Termites are common in Tennessee. A separate termite inspection (about $75-$100) is highly recommended. Many lenders require it.

### Moisture and Mold
Tennessee's humid climate creates conditions for mold growth. Check:
- Crawl spaces for moisture
- Basement walls for efflorescence
- Bathrooms for ventilation
- Attic for proper ventilation

### HVAC Demands
Tennessee summers are hot and humid, winters can be cold. Ensure:
- HVAC system is appropriately sized
- Ductwork is in good condition
- System is less than 15-20 years old

## After the Inspection

### Understanding Your Report
Your inspector will provide a detailed report, typically within 24-48 hours. It will categorize issues as:
- **Safety hazards**: Must be addressed
- **Major defects**: Significant repair costs
- **Minor defects**: Normal maintenance items

### Negotiating Repairs
Use the inspection report to:
1. Request repairs before closing
2. Ask for credits toward closing costs
3. Negotiate a price reduction
4. Walk away if issues are too significant

## Red Flags to Watch

These issues warrant serious consideration:
- Active water intrusion
- Structural damage
- Outdated electrical (knob and tube)
- Polybutylene plumbing
- Significant mold presence
- Unpermitted additions

## Final Thoughts

Never waive your inspection contingency to win a bidding war. The few hundred dollars spent on inspection can save you tens of thousands in unexpected repairs.
